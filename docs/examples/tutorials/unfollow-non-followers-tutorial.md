1. **Run `--export-only` first** â€” Before unfollowing anyone, export the non-followers list and review it. You might discover accounts you want to keep following even though they don't follow back (news outlets, public figures, etc.).

2. **Do 50â€“100 per session, not all at once** â€” X is more lenient with gradual cleanup. Running 3 sessions of 50 unfollows with 30-minute gaps between is safer than blasting through 150 in one go.

3. **Pair this with "Detect Unfollowers" for ongoing hygiene** â€” Run [detect-unfollowers](../detect-unfollowers.md) weekly to catch new non-followers as they appear, rather than letting them pile up again.

4. **Check your ratio after cleanup** â€” If you started at 2,100/890 (0.42x) and removed 1,210 non-followers, you'll be at 890/890 (1.0x). That's a massive algorithm signal â€” X shows your tweets to more people when your ratio is healthy.

5. **The "Follows you" badge may lag** â€” If someone followed you in the last few minutes, the badge might not render yet. The script reads what's currently visible on the DOM. Wait a few seconds after page load for accuracy.

- **Mutuals are never touched** â€” The script only unfollows accounts where the "Follows you" badge is **absent**. Your mutual follows are safe.
